# Final Reflection

ChatGPT helped me turn a broad and unfamiliar systems engineering task into a
small project that I could understand and explain. The most helpful part was
using AI to narrow the problem: instead of building a complicated image analysis
tool, I built a simpler accessibility audit for chart metadata. That made the
project easier to test and easier to connect to systems engineering ideas like
requirements, verification, and stakeholder needs. AI was also useful for
reviewing edge cases, such as low-contrast colors, missing labels, unreadable
font sizes, and vague alt text.

The least helpful part was that AI sometimes suggested features that were too
large for the scope of the final project. A risk of relying on AI for code
review and commit messages is that the output can sound polished even when the
logic is incomplete. I would structure a team AI playbook so AI can draft tests,
summaries, and review comments, but humans must approve the requirement choices,
security assumptions, and merge decisions. What surprised me about AI-assisted
conflict resolution was that the hard part was not editing the file; it was
choosing a policy that I could defend.
